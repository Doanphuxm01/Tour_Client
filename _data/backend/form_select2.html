Frequently Asked Questions

What type of Facebook sources can I use for the feed?
You can use Facebook page of profile to display on your website. For a page, you simply need to enter its URL or ID in the box on Source tab, and for a profile you will need to get Facebook access token first.
Which types of content can I display in the feed?
Adding Facebook Feed to website allows for showing three types of Facebook content: photos, videos, and posts. You can create a photo gallery or a news feed if you restrict the type of content shown.
Can I change the look of the header?
The header of Facebook Feed widget is absolutely flexible and allows to show or hide cover and page picture, as well as add Like and Share buttons and even show likes count. In case you want to show the feed with no header at all, you can easily switch it off. To make necessary adjustments, go to Layout tab and choose Header - you are free to customize it!
Can I restrict the type of content to show?
Yes, you can do that. You can set website Facebook widget to show only those types of content that you need. For this, go to Layout tab and enable or disable each of the content types. Thus you can show, for example, only photos, or photos and videos, or any other combination.
What elements can popup show?
Facebook Feed on website offers adjustable popup, which shows the author, likes count, share buttons and comments and may as well hide all of these elements, if you choose not to show them.
Can I show Facebook Feed widget in my language?
Facebook widget for website has been translated to 16 most popular languages and you only need to go to More tab and choose yours. If your language is not on the list and you are ready to help with translation, we will be happy to use it and add the new language to our widget.
What types of widget can I construct using Elfsight Facebook Feed?

All our widgets have fully responsive design and adaptable interface settings, which enable configuring almost any arrangement of the widget:

  • Facebook Video Feed – creating a video gallery on your website with the help of your social media account.
  • Facebook Page widget – displaying all or selected content from your profile.
  • Facebook Event Feed – creating event calendar on your site.
  • Facebook Hashtag widget - embedding on your website all posts by desired hashtags.
  • Facebook Wall Feed – adding content from the profile feed.
  • Facebook Gallery widget – shaping photo and video gallery for your site.
  • Facebook Album & Photo Feed widget – displaying single photos or whole portfolios.
  • Facebook Timeline Feed – embedding posts from page of profile feed.
  • Facebook News Feed – adding newsfeed on your website.
  • Facebook Web widget – a cloud solution for content displaying.
  • Facebook Post widget – using any posts from the social media on your website.
  • Facebook Profile widget – embedding your Facebook profile on any website page.
  • Facebook Social widget – all social content in one place.
  • Facebook Live widget – displaying posts from your profile and page.
  • Facebook Group widget – a possibility to display content from.
  • Facebook Activity Feed – unfortunately, due to changes in Facebook API, this feature is temporarily unavailable.
